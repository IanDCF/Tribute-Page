Chronology

  • 1844 - Friederich Nietzsche is born in Rocken, Germany

  • 1849 - Death of his father, a Lutheran pastor, on July 30th

  • 1850 - Family moves to Naumburg

  • 1858 - Attends boarding school at Schulpforta

  • 1864 - Studies classical philiology at Bonn University

  • 1869 - Taught classical philiology at the University of Basel, Switzerland

  • 1872 - Publishes The Birth of Tragedy out of the Spirit of Music, his first book

  • 1878 - Human, All-Too-Human is published

  • 1879 - Resigns from university with a pension

  • 1882 - Publication of The Gay Science

  • 1883 - Writes Part 1 and 2 of Thus Spake Zarathustra

  • 1884 - Publishes Part 3 of Thus Spake Zarathustra

  • 1885 - Finished last part of Thus Spake Zarathustra

  • 1886 - Writes Beyond Good and Evil

  • 1887 - Publication of Toward a Genealogy of Morals

  • 1889 - Nietzsche experiences a mental break-down and is committed to the asylum in Jena

  • 1895 - Writes The Antischrist and The Will to Power

  • 1897 - His mother dies and his sister moves him to Weimar

  • 1900 - Friederich Nietzsche dies in Weimar

  • 1904 - His sister publishes some 400 of his notes in Volume XV of The Will to Power

    - Friederich Nietzsche, Beyond Good and Evil
